Kazi Nazrul Islam

1899-1976

The Rebel Poet

Kazi Nazrul Islamwidely known as the "Rebel Poet" (Bidrohi Kobi), was a legendary Bengali poet, writer, musician, and revolutionary whose works inspired millions with their bold messages of justice, freedom, and humanity. Born on May 25, 1899, in Churulia, West Bengal, Nazrul rose from a life of poverty to become one of the most influential figures in Bengali literature and culture. His iconic poem Bidrohi (The Rebel), written in 1921, expressed powerful defiance against all forms of oppression and earned him his famous title. Beyond poetry, Nazrul wrote songs, essays, novels, and plays, contributing over 4,000 songs—now known as Nazrul Geeti—that blend classical, folk, and spiritual themes. He was a strong voice for the unity of Hindus and Muslims and stood for equality, social justice, and anti-colonialism. His writings often led to imprisonment, but his spirit remained unbroken. In 1942, he suffered a severe illness that silenced him for the rest of his life. After the independence of Bangladesh, he was brought to Dhaka and declared the National Poet of Bangladesh. He passed away on August 29, 1976, but his legacy as a fearless voice of rebellion and a champion of human dignity continues to inspire generations across Bengal and beyond.
